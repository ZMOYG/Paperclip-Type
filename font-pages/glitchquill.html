Glitch Quill

The idea for this display font originated while wondering what quill calligraphy would look like in a hyper-modern aesthetic. Glitch Quill consists solely of capital letters, numbers, & the ampersand, and is designed primarily for use in titles and drop caps. While it has no lowercase form, many of the glyphs have two variations (accessible by changing between upper and lowercase), which should help the letterforms mix and match more elegantly in different contexts.

Ethernet was developed in the 1970s and standardized in 1983 as IEEE 802.3. It has since evolved to support higher bit rates, more devices, and longer cable lengths while maintaining backward compatibility. Most homes use copper Ethernet cables, but fiber optic and coaxial options exist for faster or more advanced setups. Ethernet cables typically contain eight wires, organized into four pairs to minimize crosstalk and ensure stable data transmission.
